Privacy Policy

Your habits are
private by design.

This Privacy Policy describes how ONIS Labs handles information when you use the ONIS iPhone and Apple Watch app. The short version: we collect almost nothing, keep everything on your device, and never sell or share your data.

Effective Date

Effective date: May 21, 2026  ·  Last updated: May 21, 2026

When this policy changes materially, we update this page and revise the "Last updated" date. Continued use of ONIS after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Privacy at a glance

  • No account or registration is required to use ONIS.
  • Your habit data is stored on your device. ONIS does not sync it to iCloud, CloudKit, or any server.
  • We do not operate any servers that store user habit data.
  • We do not run ads, ad trackers, or third-party analytics SDKs.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with anyone.
  • ONIS does not connect to Apple Health (HealthKit).

1. Information we collect

ONIS does not collect or require personal information to function. The data the app creates is generated entirely by you. Specifically, ONIS stores:

  • Habit content you create: habit names, descriptions, emoji, and color choices.
  • Habit completion data: timestamps of when you mark a habit complete.
  • Streak and history data: current streak counts, completion history, and milestone records.
  • Reminder preferences: the times and days you configure for optional notifications.
  • App settings: display preferences, sort order, and configuration choices.

We do not collect your name, email address, location, contacts, photos, or any other personal data unless you voluntarily include that content in a habit name or description.

2. How your data is collected and stored

All data listed above is collected directly from your interactions with the ONIS app. No data is collected passively or in the background beyond what iOS and watchOS require to run the app (e.g., standard system crash logs managed by Apple).

On-device storage: Habit data is stored locally on your iPhone and Apple Watch using Apple's standard on-device storage frameworks.

Device-to-device sync: When you use ONIS on both iPhone and Apple Watch, the two devices sync directly with each other using Apple's WatchConnectivity framework. This happens device-to-device — your data does not pass through iCloud, CloudKit, our servers, or any external service.

3. How we use your data

The data ONIS stores is used for one purpose: to provide the habit tracking functionality you expect. Specifically:

  • Displaying your habit list, progress rings, and streak counts in the app.
  • Sending local notifications for reminders you configure (processed entirely on-device by iOS).
  • Syncing your data directly between your own iPhone and Apple Watch (device-to-device), with no cloud involved.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, model training, or any purpose other than running the app for you.

4. Apple Health (HealthKit)

ONIS does not request, read, or write Apple Health (HealthKit) data. ONIS works only from the habits you log yourself and does not connect to the Health app.

5. Storage and sync

ONIS stores your data on your device. It does not use iCloud, CloudKit, or any external cloud or server to store or sync your habit data. When you use ONIS on both iPhone and Apple Watch, the two devices sync directly with each other (device-to-device) via Apple's WatchConnectivity framework — your data does not pass through any cloud or third party.

If you use Apple's standard device backup, your ONIS data is included in that backup like any other app's data. That backup is managed by Apple under your Apple ID, not by ONIS, and we cannot access it.

6. Third-party services

ONIS does not include or integrate any of the following:

  • Third-party advertising networks or ad SDKs.
  • Third-party analytics platforms (e.g., Mixpanel, Amplitude, Firebase Analytics, Segment).
  • Third-party crash reporting tools beyond standard Apple crash logs.
  • Social network SDKs (e.g., Facebook, X/Twitter, TikTok).
  • External databases that receive user habit content.

The only external service ONIS may rely on is Apple's own App Analytics — aggregate, anonymous crash and usage data managed by Apple, not us (see Section 7). ONIS does not use iCloud, CloudKit, or any server for your habit data.

7. Analytics and crash reports

ONIS may receive aggregate, anonymized analytics through Apple's App Analytics — a service Apple provides to app developers. This data is managed entirely by Apple, does not identify individual users, and we cannot cross-reference it with your habit data. Whether Apple shares this data depends on the privacy settings you configure in your iPhone Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements.

We have no other analytics instrumentation in the app.

8. Data retention and how to delete your data

Your habit data persists until you choose to delete it. There is no automatic expiry. Here is how to delete it:

Delete all habit data within the app:

  • Open ONIS on your iPhone
  • Go to Settings (gear icon)
  • Tap Reset All Data
  • Confirm — this immediately deletes all habits, completions, and streak history from your device

Delete everything completely:

  • First reset all data using the step above
  • Then delete the ONIS app from your iPhone and Apple Watch

You may also email support@onis.club to request data deletion assistance. Since your data is on your device (not our servers), we will guide you through the steps above.

9. Revoking consent and controlling access

You can revoke any permission ONIS has been granted at any time:

  • Disable notifications: iPhone Settings → ONIS → Notifications → toggle off Allow Notifications.

Revoking any of the above permissions does not delete previously stored data. See Section 8 for deletion instructions.

10. Children's privacy

ONIS is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has used ONIS and provided personal information, please contact us at support@onis.club and we will assist with deletion.

11. Your rights — GDPR, CCPA, and international users

European Economic Area (EEA) and UK — GDPR: If you are located in the EEA or UK, you have the right to: access personal data we hold about you; request correction or deletion; object to or restrict processing; and data portability. Because ONIS stores your data on your own device, most of these rights are exercisable directly by you through the controls described in Sections 8 and 9.

California — CCPA: California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to delete, and the right to opt out of sale. We do not sell personal information. To exercise any CCPA rights, contact us at support@onis.club.

Other jurisdictions: We comply with applicable local privacy laws. Contact us if you have questions about your rights in your jurisdiction.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the app or applicable law. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. If changes are significant, we may also notify users within the app. Your continued use of ONIS after the update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

13. Contact us

If you have questions, concerns, or requests about this Privacy Policy or your data, please contact ONIS Labs:

Email: support@onis.club

We typically respond within 48 hours.
